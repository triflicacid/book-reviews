Review

This is a slice-of-life style series that follows young cultivator Wu Ying through his journey to get stronger. As such, this book is rather slow-paced. The writing and content themselves are excellent, with the author's research and immersion in Chinese culture shining throughout the books, but is ultimatley too ploddy and lacking any real goal or action for my liking. As this tier list is on my overall enthralment and engagement with a series, and not objective on the book itself, it scores average; it is not boring, but doesn't make me want to read more.

Magic System

Cultivators are those able and skilled enought to absorb and cycles essence from their surroundings through their cores. In the Body Cleansing stage, a cultivator unblocks their meridians which allow essence to flow around their body. In later stages, they create an energy store and core within themselves able to hold essence, granting them the ability to project essence rather than simply use it to strengthen the body.

Everyone has a Dao which they hunt for during their cultivation journey, which at later stages is vital the the future of their path and what magic they can perform.

Main Characters

  • Wu Ying - the main character, originally a peasent rice farmer. He is recruited into the army but is unable to fight due to a sword injury. He helps save their men by spotting an attempted sneak-attack on the camp. This earns a notice from an Elder of the Verdant Green Waters sect, to which he is recruited. Unlike many other series in this tier list, Wu Ying is not particularly gifted and does not posess any skill ot ability which marks him out; rather, he is a standard, but relatable, man with a drive to learn and progress.
  • Tou He - an ex-monk who becomes Wu Ying's friend. Tou is gifted and finds progressing and fighting easy, but is rather laid-back much to the chagrin of Wu.
  • Yin Xue - a lore's son, the nobleman has it in for Wu Ying and injured him in a sword duel. They both get invited to the Waters sect. Yin fights Wu for a spot in the inner circle but loses when Wu severaly injured him after Yin refuses to yield.
  • Elder Yang - a beautiful female Elder which plenty of student drool over, she becomes friends with Wu as she is a member of his recruiting party. She often mets him in his house to ask for his feedback and Wu asks her for advice and guidance.
  • Li Yao - a martial art specialist inner-sect member. Accompanies Wu on the expedition to harvest a rare flower, where she is empressed by his confidence and become shis girlfriend.
